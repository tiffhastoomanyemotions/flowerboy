4.19.19
there was once a time,
a few windings of the watch ago,
that i wanted to save the world.

and then i learned that the world is not my playground,
but rather the corroding ticking gears of a political bureaucracy
i’d rather not touch.

i’d rather save my world.
save the fun and entertainment, bubbling from my eyes.
save the nihilistic mischief, grinning from heart to lungs.

i live for the tears of thought.
i live for the soft aesthetic.
i live for the dying apple, bleeding through and through.

4.1.19
i almost crashed my car cloud-gazing

two whales
swimming side by side in the clouds
were beautifully, perfectly, captivatingly “human.”

the visual of compartmentalized multifacetedness
and the ever-shifting space-time
that rarely offsets the wind’s intentions.

i saw in them the bitter balance between
being unapologetically yourself,
and being loved.

3.28.19
i’d fall for me like rain on pavement.
i’d wait for me until the 5am.

we spend so much time
looking for someone to love

that we forget the ones we should love the most
are ourselves.

so plug the stars back in,
wipe the clouds from the roof,

let’s watch the moonflowers bloom
until our dreams bid farewell.

3.10.19
i think the music you listen to
says a lot about who you are.

when i was 10 years old,
i flipped through radio stations
until i found a ballad.
because ballads were sad
and so was i.
that was the year
i started crying myself to sleep.

when i was 13 years old,
i scrolled through youtube
until i found millennium.
because dancey edm was electric
and i wanted to feel something other than numb.
that was the year
i started hurting myself.

now i’m seventeen.
i hit shuffle on my spotify
not caring which song plays.
because all of it is k-
reminding me that it's okay to be happy.
this is the year
i finally learn to love who i am.
