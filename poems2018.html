11.24.18
selfportraits

a girl with golden skin and onyx hair
a tattered inky kimono pooling around her waist
she’s sitting one knee up, guarding a viny torii
a sheathed katana beside her ground-tapping fingers

a boy with baby soft skin and ruby red lips, kohl around his eyes
worn out boots caressing baby feet, black cap shading rosy cheeks
cross-legged, he’s comfortable among the flowers and the stems
look, the ferns are hugging him, whispering “be my friend”

a white-haired, white-skinned, wide-eyed moonchild
never worn anything but plain white t shirt, jeans, and sneakers
his tilted chin rests on gentle palms, crossed ankles on roof tiles
he looks at you—through you—he only has eyes for the stars

a city kid, living fast, smog in his lungs and culture in his veins
a glowing silver watch under dull gray sleeves to keep track of the trains, two a day
his sleepy eyes and smiling lips rest atop a dusty backpack
he will forever belong to the painted concrete, rumbling and bright

a cotton candy girl living only in a bubblegum world
hair gathered in pigtails, wearing pink everything pink
she’s got her leg flicked back and her palms flat down like a princess
smile so big she can’t see any of your negativity

a pale scrawny boy, freckles tracing constellations on his cheeks
big round glasses indent his nose and he’s still wearing his school uniform
sitting at an old walnut desk, protected by his castle of books
what’s he writing back there? perhaps this very scrawl

10.24.18
if you strained your eyes
against the lens of the Hubble

perhaps you’d be able to gaze
upon the blueshift of the planets.

just like pluto’s tendency toward blue
as it finds itself hurtling at neptune,

the castles around my bed
and the cicatrices in my flesh

are tinged blue
from my failed endeavors out of loneliness.

i think that if you instead closed your eyes
and listened with all your heart

you’d be able to hear, maybe even feel,
the blueshift of humans.

10.10.18
i might as well have burned their pages

buying paper terrariums
of ink and universes,

leaving them on a ledge
to grow dust instead of thoughts.

if you ask me why they sit
instead of flip,

i’d tell you it’s the watch’s fault
for swinging hands too fast.

really, it’s just that time flies
when you neglect to lock its cage.
